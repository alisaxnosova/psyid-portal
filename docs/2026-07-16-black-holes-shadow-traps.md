# 2026-07-16 — Black Holes: frank shadow-traps in the adult report

Draft for the methodology. Proposed as an extension of §08 (The shadow / growth edge),
with a required scoping edit to §13 (Ethics). Not yet in the whitepaper.

## The idea

Every profile gets named **Black Holes** — the specific ways this person fails when they are
*not* operating as their best self. Not softened, not hedged, not reframed as a hidden strength.
A black hole is the trap you fall into precisely because you cannot see it from the inside.

Where the growth edge says *"here is what to develop,"* a black hole says *"here is what you
actually do at 2am on a bad week, and here is who it costs."*

The report should be frank enough that reading it stings a little. That is the product.

## Why frankness is a methodological argument, not just a tone preference

This is the strongest case for it, and it belongs in the whitepaper:

**A report made only of strengths is a Barnum instrument.** Flattering, vaguely positive
descriptions are accepted as accurate by nearly everyone regardless of their actual scores
(the Forer effect). That acceptance is worthless as evidence — it means the text does not
*discriminate*. It makes an unvalidated instrument *look* valid while measuring nothing.

Frank, specific failure modes are **falsifiable**. "You re-litigate a conversation nobody else
remembers" only rings true for some profiles and lands flat for others. Text that can be *wrong*
is text whose being-right is informative. So black holes:

1. **raise discriminant validity** of the reader's experience — the report stops fitting everyone;
2. give the §12 validation study something meaningful to test (do people endorse their own
   black holes more than another profile's? that is a real, runnable experiment);
3. buy the trust that makes §08's growth edge actionable, because the reader can tell we are
   not selling them a horoscope.

Politeness, here, is a validity problem — not a kindness.

## Where it sits in the architecture

Level **2/3** (McAdams). A black hole is an **interpretation**, never a measurement. It gets no
code slot, no band, no confidence interval. §03's rule holds without exception: the axis positions
may be defended as data; the black hole may not. It is derived from the five axes — it is not a
sixth.

## How it derives

Two sources, both already in the model:

- **Over-extension of a dominant pole.** At bands 4–5 a strength inverts into its own liability.
  `D5` reliability becomes paralysis when the plan breaks. `L5` clear judgment becomes a person
  who wins the argument and loses the room. The black hole lives at the *far* end of the axis,
  not the near one — which is why it needs the band, not just the pole.
- **The shadow / least-developed axis** (§08) — the underdeveloped opposite, which "operates in a
  primitive, often disruptive way outside conscious control" (Jung's inferior function). This is
  where a person is least skilled and least aware, so it is where the trap is invisible.

Practical consequence: **black holes are band-gated.** A near-balanced axis (band 0–1) has no
black hole worth naming — there is no over-extension to invert. Only bands 3+ earn one.

## Age policy — a hard line

| | Adult (PsyID, 15–45) | Youth (PsyID Youth, 8–14) |
|---|---|---|
| Register | **Frank.** Named traps, real consequences, no cushioning. | **Strengths-based, developmental.** |
| Reader | The person themselves. | A **parent**, reading about their child. |
| Black holes | Yes — the point of the report. | **No.** Growth edge only, per §08/§13. |

The youth product is untouched by this proposal. A child is not told they have a black hole, and
a parent is never handed a dark verdict on their kid. §08's existing rule stands there verbatim.

## The limits that still hold (and why)

Frank is not the same as unfalsifiable insult. Four constraints survive:

1. **No clinical vocabulary.** *Neurotic, unstable, anxious, disorder* stay banned — not for
   gentleness, but because ReNo is **empirically unvalidated** (§00). A clinical-sounding claim is
   one we cannot defend, from an instrument that has not earned the right to make it. This is a
   liability line, not a tone line.
2. **Describe behaviour and cost, never character.** "You go quiet and let the resentment compound
   for three weeks" is frank and true-or-false. "You are cowardly" is a verdict, unfalsifiable, and
   indefensible from a questionnaire.
3. **It must be actionable.** A black hole the reader cannot recognise or do anything about is
   just cruelty with a radar chart attached.
4. **Never presented as measured.** §03. The sting is narrative; the data is the position.

## ⚠️ This is not purely additive — §13 must be edited

§13 currently reads: *"The shadow is framed as a growth edge, never a deficit — especially for a
child."* The "especially" implies a general rule with a child-specific emphasis. Black holes
contradict that general rule for adults.

**Required edit:** scope it explicitly — *"For the youth product, the shadow is framed as a growth
edge, never a deficit. For the adult product, the shadow is additionally surfaced as named black
holes (§08b), with the clinical-vocabulary ban unchanged."*

Without that edit the whitepaper contradicts itself, and the contradiction is exactly the kind a
research collaborator will find first.

## Open questions for you

- **Naming.** "Black hole" is vivid and Level-3 appropriate. It is also unrecoverable and
  inescapable by metaphor — which may overshoot. Worth deciding whether the metaphor promises
  something the content should not.
- **How many per profile?** One per over-extended axis could mean four. Recommend a cap (2–3,
  the highest-band axes) so the report does not become a pile-on.
- **Tier placement.** Black holes are the most valuable page in the report. Basic tier or Full only?
- **Where does the sting land?** Directly after the strengths section reads as a rug-pull; a
  dedicated late section reads as honest. Recommend the latter.
