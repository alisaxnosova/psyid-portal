Your type

A quick read on how you're wired

You're an ideas-first connector. Where others see a to-do list, you see possibility — and you're happiest when a plan is still loose enough to improve. That openness is your engine: it lets you spot the angle nobody else noticed and rally people around it.

Because your decisions run through values before logic, you read a room fast and adjust. People trust you quickly. The same warmth that makes you magnetic can make it hard to say no — which is exactly why your growth plan leans on protecting your focus.

Growth plan

A few things to try

1

A soft deadline

Put a gentle deadline on the tasks that drag on. For you, a limit frees rather than confines — it turns open-ended energy into a finished thing.

2

A quiet hour

Thirty minutes a day with no notifications. Your ideas need a pause to ripen; the noise is stealing your best thinking.

3

Name the trade-off

Before a values-led call, say the logical cost out loud. You'll still choose with your heart — just with both eyes open.

4

The rule of three

Hold no more than three live projects. The fourth goes on a waiting list — protecting your focus is the whole game.
